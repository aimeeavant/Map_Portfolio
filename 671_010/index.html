American Viticultural Areas in San Luis Obispo County, California

Authored by: Aimee Armour-Avant

An American Viticultural Area, or AVA, is a specific type of appellation of origin used on wine labels. An AVA is a delimited grape-growing region with specific geographic or climatic features that distinguish it from the surrounding regions and affect how grapes are grown. Using an AVA designation on a wine label allows vintners to describe more accurately the origin of their wines to consumers and helps consumers identify wines they may purchase.

This map shows the AVAs in the county of San Luis Obispo in the state of California. The area has three major AVAs and a number of sub-AVAs.

This map was made using the following data sources:

  • County boundary: County of San Luis Obispo
  • Roads: US Census 2019 Tiger/Line Shapefiles: Roads
  • Highways: California State Geoportal: National Highway System
  • Shape files for AVAs: Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau
  • Vineyards: Open Street Map
